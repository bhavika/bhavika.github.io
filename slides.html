Teaching
As a proponent of code literacy and interdisciplinary research, I spend Friday mornings teaching workshops on Python, ArcGIS & QGIS at the Digital Scholarship Center (DiSC) within George Mason University. These workshops assume little to no knowledge of programming or CS concepts. I aim to demonstrate how simple programming concepts can help doctoral students, faculty & undergrads take the first step towards conducting reproducible research efficiently through the use of open source data analysis tools. These workshops are attended by faculty & students from departments like Data Analytics, Political Science, Nursing, Geospatial Science to name a few.
